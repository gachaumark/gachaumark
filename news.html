Breaking News

Strike Day 1 – Chaos as matatus stay off roads

June 1, 2026

Commuters across major routes faced an uphill task getting to work this morning as operators grounded their vehicles unexpectedly.

The strike, which caught thousands by surprise, stems from disputes over newly introduced county transit levies and police harassment claims.

Major hubs like Thika Road, Jogoo Road, and Ngong Road were completely deserted of the usual multi-colored buses, leaving only private vehicles and walking commuters.

Commuter Impact

Commuter Impact – Thousands stranded across Nairobi

June 1, 2026

With options limited, thousands of residents resorted to walking long distances, while fare prices for alternative means skyrocketed.

Boda-boda operators capitalised on the crisis, tripling their usual rates. A trip that normally costs KSh 100 surged to over KSh 300, forcing many to trek on foot from as far as Kasarani to the CBD.

Employers have been urged to be lenient with late arrivals as the city's transport backbone remains crippled.

Government Action

Government Response – Matatu Owners Association meets Transport CS

June 2, 2026

The Ministry of Transport has called for an emergency stakeholder meeting to address the grievances raised by the operators.

The Cabinet Secretary opened the closed-door session by acknowledging the vital role the matatu sector plays in the economy while stressing that illegal blockades will not be tolerated.

Representatives from the Matatu Owners Association (MOA) presented a 5-point memorandum demanding immediate policy reviews regarding digital compliance tracking.

Resolution

Strike Resolution – Services resume after deal reached

June 2, 2026

Good news for commuters as the national strike has officially been called off following a successful compromise between parties.

A joint communique confirmed that enforcement of the contentious digital levies would be suspended for 60 days to allow for broader public participation.

Vehicles are already returning to their designated routes, and traffic flow is expected to normalize fully by tomorrow morning.
